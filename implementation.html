The Four Implementation Phases

Phase 1: Align

Week 1

▼

What Happens:

  • Kickoff call with steering committee (both sides)
  • Align on success metrics (adoption, activity, outcomes)
  • Establish North Star framework for build
"Alignment is key. First order of business, once we take care of commercials and signatures, there's a little bit of a lead time to assign a resource to any project, which is why obviously the sooner we have a decision, the better."
— Danny Kisch

Success Metrics Options:

  • Rep adoption (80%+ benchmark)
  • Activity-based metrics (emails, calls, account saturation)
  • Pipeline generation
  • Sequence performance

Phase 2: Design

Weeks 2-3

▼

What Happens:

Workflow Design Interviews (the heart of implementation)
  • 30-60 minute conversations with top reps/team leads
  • One interview per unique user group
  • Document: day-in-the-life, click paths, quick wins, critical priorities
"Workflow design is the heart and soul of it. We would schedule these like probably within the first week after kickoff just so that we can really understand and do our due diligence into what makes your team tick."
— Danny Kisch

What Gets Captured:

  • Current Salesloft workflows
  • Pain points to solve immediately
  • Team-specific priorities (SDR vs AE vs CS)
  • Integration touchpoints
  • Content migration priorities

For MVP:

Priority workflows get built first. Non-critical features intentionally deferred to Phase 2.

"We can prioritize those workflows, those methods that are more important for the business that need to be onboarded sooner rather than later. And then we can intentionally plan to come back afterwards and flush out the rest of the experience."
— Danny Kisch

Phase 3: Mobilize

Week 3-4

▼

What Happens:

  • CRM integration buildout
  • Governance structure implementation
  • Content migration (sequences, templates, snippets)
  • User provisioning and access controls

Salesforce Team Involvement:

Critical Clarification from Danny:
"Two weeks is plenty of time. I have done this in a couple of days. I've done it in one day."
— Danny Kisch
What Salesforce Admin Actually Does:
  • Initial setup: 2 minutes (authentication)
  • Configuration: 1-2 days maximum
  • Ongoing maintenance: Zero unless major CRM changes
Why This is Different Than Expected:

Outreach's Salesforce integration is purpose-built for minimal admin lift. This isn't a bolt-on integration—it's an enterprise-grade bidirectional sync that handles complexity automatically.

Phase 4: Execute

Week 4 (MVP) or Week 5-6 (Standard)

▼

What Happens:

Training (Workshop-Style):
  • Hour 1: Show and tell
  • Hour 2: Hands-on with live tasks
  • Reps use the system during training

Result: Zero post-training learning curve

"We want to make sure that all of our reps are adopted. Our benchmark for success is at least 80% rep adoption post launch."
— Danny Kisch's Approach

For CINC Specifically:

  • Sales team launch: January 1
  • Client Services teams: Phased or simultaneous (TBD based on Chelsea/Danielle input)
  • Managed Services begins: Handles advanced features without internal lift
